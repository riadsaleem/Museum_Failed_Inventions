Weird Inventions

The most bizarre and hilarious inventions that somehow made it to production

1957-1960

Ford Edsel

The car that became synonymous with commercial failure. Despite massive marketing efforts and a $250 million investment, the Edsel's unusual design, reliability issues, and high price tag during a recession made it one of automotive history's biggest flops. Only lasted 3 years in production.

$250M Lost 3 Years Epic Fail
2012-2015

Google Glass

The future of wearable technology that arrived too early. Privacy concerns, social stigma ("Glassholes"), technical limitations, and a $1,500 price tag turned this revolutionary idea into a cautionary tale about timing in tech innovation.

Privacy Issues $1,500 Social Stigma
1996-1997

McDonald's Arch Deluxe

The "burger with the grown-up taste" that nobody wanted. With a promotional budget of $150-200 million (the largest in fast food history), McDonald's tried to attract adults but forgot that people go to McDonald's for cheap, familiar food, not upscale dining.

$200M Marketing Wrong Audience Customer Reject
1985

New Coke

What do you do when you sell the world's most popular soft drink? Change the formula, of course! Coca-Cola's attempt to compete with Pepsi backfired spectacularly. Customer outrage was so intense that news anchor Peter Jennings interrupted General Hospital to announce the return of "Classic" Coke.

Public Outrage 79 Days Formula Reverted
1930s

Urban Window Baby Cage

Dubbed by Time as one of the "50 worst inventions," this contraption was used to give city babies fresh air by dangling them outside apartment windows. Doctors believed this would boost immune systems, but the obvious safety risks made this a short-lived trend.

Baby Endangerment Safety Risk Quickly Banned
1981

Siamese Dancing Shoes

Got two left feet? These connected dancing shoes were designed to help couples stay in sync on the dance floor. Featured in the spoof "Better Living Catalogue," they were more likely to cause couples to trip spectacularly than to dance gracefully.

Connected Shoes Trip Hazard Comedy Gold
